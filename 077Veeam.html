Who is Veeam?

What Do They Do?

Posted by Scott Bollinger / @kfalconspb on May 25, 2018 #Virtualization

Back IT up

Probably best known for it's backup and recovery products, Veeam has positioned itself in the last twelve years, as a market leader in the availability space. Servicing 300K customers, large and small, as well as having 50K ProPartners.

Meh-arketing Speak

Marketing speak, usually means little to front line operations. If you've ever been in charge of backups at any organization, then you already know, its a mostly thankless job. Sure, you may get an odd thank you, from an end user, if you even, talk directly to users anymore. However, should something not be up to snuff, a failed backup on the wrong day, or the backup's not recoverable. Which could end up being a resume generating event.

What I want? #PeaceSleepTacos

The best feeling for a backup administrator, even more so, for those in IT with many hats, is to know that everything in your environment, that is important, and should be backed up, is.

Beyond that, knowing that the backups actually work, and you're able to recover what's needed. But remember, it's not just files, but whole servers. What about databases, or particular tables, and exchange? What about Active Directory objects, or that odd physical server you haven't been able to virtualize yet?

Availability

When viewed from the users point of view, the recovery of a resource, it's configuration, and any related data, should be thoughtless. Like electricity, no one thinks about it, until it's out. Then people go insane until it's restored. Minutes seem like hours, as our routines are thrown into turmoil.

Through the years, the electric companies, have come up with redundancies, and backups to make their systems seemingly thoughtless. It's always available. Barring wide spread natural disasters, you'll usually be back up and running in a matter of minutes to a couple hours.

Data is the the same way. If you need an answer to a question, we don't know, we google it. Need that picture of a whiteboard from five business trips ago, you access your picture storage. It's always there and available. This didn't exist 15 years ago, need a picture, than you had to physically go to your photo album. Like the electric industry, IT has had to come up with protection, backup, and availability solutions for data as well. Availability, it's just not your traditional hierarchical backup plan of daily, weekly, monthly anymore. What about the minute to minute?

I've PoC'd, tried, bought, and was disappointed, in many products before I finally found Veeam. At it's most basic, it just worked. It didn't require a full time employee to setup and manage and I could finally sleep, when the need arose.

So if you need to backup your virtualization environment, SQL, Exchange, Active Directory, and physical machines, do your due diligence, but Veeam should definitely be one of the products you should review, and will mostly likely compare other products against later.
