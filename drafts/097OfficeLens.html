Microsoft Office Len

I've been meaning to write this post for a while. When introduced to Microsoft Office Lens a couple of years ago at a conference. I used to chuckle at those attendee that used to take pictures of every presentation slide. I always figured that the slides where usually available after the show. But then I started taking a few photos to take notes and reminders for myself. As I would never remember what I wanted or where in the presentation the slide is I wanted to remember. Then as I started using twitter more I'd comment or share information (and sarcasm) as I found it. If you're like me there are other time this method of note taking is handy. Whiteboards at work. Presentations with vendors. So for starters I'd take photos of the whiteboard or slide. And depending where you where sitting and the angle the object would look weird and disoriented. If only there was an app that allowed you to take a picture and it would automatically adjust for angle, lighting and clear up the content making more readable and noteworthy. Well there is it's called Microsoft Office Lens. This application allows you to select if the object you are take a photo of if its a whiteboard, document, business card of photo. This initial selection helps the app to determine initial setting that helps adjust in those certain situations. But don't worry, the photo option allows you to take a photo from your library and run the process on a photo that was already taken. The process is simple as well. Make a selection of the document type and press the shutter button, it takes the photo and does it's post processing correcting for angle, lighting, and cleaning up the text it finds. After it's processed, you accept the result or take another photo. From there you have a variety of options that allow you to save if as a photo or pdf and to which location.
